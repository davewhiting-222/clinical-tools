Accutane Treatment Guide

Your comprehensive guide to isotretinoin therapy for acne

Important Information

Accutane (isotretinoin) is a highly effective medication for severe acne. This handout provides essential information about your treatment. Please read it carefully and discuss any questions with your healthcare provider.

Why Accutane?

Accutane is prescribed for severe acne that hasn't responded adequately to other treatments, or for acne that is causing significant scarring or psychological distress. It is one of the most effective treatments available for acne.

High Success Rate

Over 85% of patients achieve long-term clearance or significant improvement after one course of treatment.

Targets Root Causes

Unlike other treatments, Accutane addresses multiple factors that contribute to acne formation.

Long-lasting Results

Many patients experience permanent or very long-lasting improvement after completing treatment.

How Accutane Works

Accutane is a vitamin A derivative that works through several mechanisms to treat acne:

Reduces oil production Dramatically decreases sebum (oil) production from your skin's oil glands, which removes the environment where acne bacteria thrive.
Prevents pore blockage Normalizes the shedding of skin cells inside pores, preventing them from becoming blocked and forming comedones (blackheads and whiteheads).
Reduces inflammation Decreases inflammation in the skin, which helps reduce redness and swelling associated with acne lesions.
Decreases bacteria By reducing oil and changing the skin environment, it indirectly reduces acne-causing bacteria.

Completion Matters

Reaching the full cumulative dose reduces the risk of acne returning after treatment.

It is important to complete the full course of treatment if possible. Studies show that patients who reach their target cumulative dose have much lower rates of acne recurrence compared to those who stop early.

What to Expect During Treatment

Weeks 1-4: Initial Phase

Your acne may initially worsen (called a "purge") as the medication brings deeper lesions to the surface. This is normal and temporary. You may start noticing drier skin and lips.

Weeks 4-8: Early Improvement

The initial flare typically subsides, and you'll begin to see reduction in new breakouts. Dryness will be more noticeable.

Weeks 8-16: Progressive Clearing

Significant improvement becomes evident. Oil production decreases noticeably. Existing acne continues to heal.

Weeks 16-24: Final Phase

Continued clearing and refinement. Skin texture improves. You'll reach your target cumulative dose during this period.

Post-Treatment

Most improvement is maintained long-term. Some patients see continued improvement even after stopping the medication.

Side Effects and Management

While Accutane is very effective, it does cause side effects in most patients. Fortunately, most are manageable with proper care.

Common Side Effects (Very Frequent)

Dry, chapped lips (nearly universal) Apply lip balm frequently throughout the day (every 1-2 hours). Keep one with you at all times. Thick ointments like Aquaphor, Vaseline, or Lanolin work best. Apply before bed.
Dry skin and mild itching Use gentle, fragrance-free moisturizers twice daily. Avoid hot showers. Consider using a humidifier at night. Apply moisturizer to damp skin after bathing.
Dry eyes Use preservative-free artificial tears several times daily. Avoid contact lenses if possible; if you must wear them, use rewetting drops frequently.
Dry nasal passages, nosebleeds Apply a thin layer of petroleum jelly or saline gel inside nostrils daily. Use a saline nasal spray. Run a humidifier, especially while sleeping.
Increased sun sensitivity Use broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily. Wear protective clothing and hats. Avoid prolonged sun exposure and tanning beds completely.

Less Common But Important Side Effects

Muscle and joint aches Usually mild. Stay well-hydrated. Consider reducing high-impact exercise. Over-the-counter pain relievers (acetaminophen) may help.
Temporary hair thinning Usually mild and reverses after treatment. Use gentle hair care products and avoid tight hairstyles.
Headaches Usually mild. Stay hydrated and ensure adequate sleep. If severe or persistent, contact your doctor immediately.

Contact Your Doctor If You Experience:

  • Severe headache, vision changes, or nausea/vomiting (may indicate rare increased pressure in the skull)
  • Severe stomach pain, rectal bleeding, or severe diarrhea (possible inflammatory bowel issues)
  • Yellowing of skin or eyes, dark urine, or severe fatigue (possible liver issues)
  • Mood changes, depression, or thoughts of self-harm
  • Severe skin reactions or allergic symptoms
  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain

Laboratory Monitoring

You will have regular blood tests to monitor liver function, cholesterol, and triglycerides. Some patients experience temporary elevations that typically resolve after treatment. Your doctor may adjust your dose if needed.

Skincare Routine While on Accutane

Your skin will be more sensitive and dry during treatment. Adapting your skincare routine is essential for comfort and optimal results.

Morning Routine

  • Cleanse with a gentle, fragrance-free, non-foaming cleanser (such as Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser or CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser)
  • Apply a rich, fragrance-free moisturizer to damp skin (such as CeraVe Moisturizing Cream or Vanicream)
  • Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen (mineral sunscreens with zinc oxide may be gentler)
  • Apply lip balm generously

Evening Routine

  • Cleanse gently with the same mild cleanser
  • Apply moisturizer to damp skin (you can use a heavier cream at night)
  • Apply thick ointment to lips before bed
  • Consider applying petroleum jelly to very dry areas

Avoid These While on Accutane:

  • Harsh scrubs, exfoliants, or peels (physical or chemical)
  • Products with retinoids, alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs), or beta-hydroxy acids (BHAs)
  • Astringent toners or alcohol-based products
  • Hot water, hot tubs, or saunas
  • Waxing (your skin is more fragile and can tear; wait 6 months after stopping Accutane)
  • Cosmetic procedures like laser treatments, dermabrasion, or deep chemical peels

Follow-Up Schedule

Regular monitoring is essential to ensure safe and effective treatment.

Initial Phase (First 2-3 months)

Monthly visits until your dose is stabilized. Each visit includes a clinical assessment, discussion of side effects, and blood work (typically done before your appointment).

Continuation Phase (Months 3-6)

Every 3 months once your dose is stable and side effects are well-managed. You'll still need regular blood tests.

Post-Treatment

A final visit approximately 4-8 weeks after completing treatment to assess your results and discuss long-term skincare maintenance.

During each visit, we will:

Review Side Effects

Monitor Labs

Adjust Dose

Additional Important Information

Take with food Accutane is best absorbed when taken with a meal containing some fat. Take it at the same time each day for consistency.
Avoid vitamin A supplements Do not take additional vitamin A, as this can increase the risk of side effects. Check multivitamins for vitamin A content.
Stay hydrated Drink plenty of water throughout the day to help with dryness and overall comfort.
Store properly Keep medication at room temperature, away from moisture and heat. Keep out of reach of children.
